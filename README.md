## - Power BI Projects

### [**Analysis of Traffic Accidents in New York City (Jan.2021 - Apr.2023)**](https://app.powerbi.com/view?r=eyJrIjoiNzczMzJmYmItMjU3My00Nzk3LTllMjAtMjk4MDRmNTNiZWRlIiwidCI6IjEyYmRlMWE1LWIwNDYtNDc5Yi1hNjM1LTk2MWVhMmI1YzA3OSJ9)

NYC Traffic Accidents Dashboard, developed using data from NYC Open Data (Jan/2021 – Apr/2023).
<img width="1535" height="864" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/c9b55d30-133b-4108-a210-a2eb14c9207d" />

### **Context and Overview**
- Exploratory and comparative analysis of traffic accidents between previous years and the current period
- KPIs covering total accidents, injuries, fatalities, monthly trends, and daily averages
- Identification of districts, streets, weekdays, and time periods with the highest accident concentrations
- Breakdown of injuries and fatalities by pedestrians, cyclists, and motorists
- Seasonal and behavioral patterns such as winter reductions, Friday peak hours, and high-risk areas (Brooklyn, Queens, Bronx)

### **Key Insights**
- From 2021 to 2022, total accidents decreased by 6.1% (303 vs. 284 accidents/day)
- 238,421 total accidents, resulting in 111,237 injuries and 601 fatalities
- Pedestrians: 18,877 injured, 286 dead
- Cyclists: 10,932 injured, 47 dead
- Motorists: 81,428 injured, 268 dead
- March had the highest number of accidents and injuries (25,099 and 11,586), while July had the highest fatalities (65)
- December recorded the lowest number of accidents (16,948), likely influenced by winter seasonality
- Fridays concentrated the most incidents, especially between 4 PM and 5 PM
- The districts with the highest accident rates: Brooklyn (32.05%), Queens (26.74%), and Bronx (17.3%)

### **Skills and Approach**
- Data cleaning and ETL using Power Query
- Advanced modeling and DAX calculations for KPIs, time intelligence, and drill-down analysis
- Custom tooltips, navigation structure, and analytical storytelling
- Data visualization best practices applied to highlight patterns and critical risk zones

<img width="1534" height="862" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/628bc930-2d2a-4d85-ada9-bdeb1b0f25e8" />



### **Context and Overview**
- Exploratory analysis of streaming platform content between 2000 and 2022
- Year-over-year breakdown of title volume, user ratings and popularity indicators
- Identification of the highest-rated years, genres and parental guidance categories
- Top 10 highest-rated titles and distribution of ratings across content categories

- Significant growth in total titles released from 2000 to 2022
- 2019 recorded the highest volume of releases: 793 titles with an average rating of 7.0

- Clear distribution of rating scores across parental guidance categories
- Genres showed distinct rating patterns, revealing which content types perform best with audiences
- Vote counts (popularity) help identify which top-rated titles also have the strongest engagement
- The Top 10 titles highlight a consistent preference for specific genres and rating ranges
